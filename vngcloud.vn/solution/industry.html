Centralized management of camera systems with optimal storage costs

Banks must have a surveillance camera system and store surveillance camera data for at least 100 days (Circular on Safety of Information Systems in Banking Operations, No. 18/2018 / TT-NHNN of State Bank of Vietnam) to serve the work of counterparty, transaction inspection, security. Banks have many branches spread across many locations, making it difficult to manage camera systems, large costs for storing content, and costs for system investment, operation and maintenance.

Looking for the most cost-effective and optimal storage and data backup plan

According to Circular 18/2018 / TT-NHNN Article 21 of the State Bank of Vietnam, Data of information systems at level 2 or higher must have an automatic backup plan suitable to the frequency of data changes and ensure the principle that arising data must be backed up within 24 hours. Backup data must be stored on external storage media (such as tape, hard disk, optical disc or other storage media) and securely stored and preserved separate from the information system installation area.

For information systems at level 2 or higher, it is required to check and restore backup data from external storage media at least every six months. Organizations that have both primary and backup information systems located outside of Vietnam must store personal information and transaction data of customers in Vietnam in accordance with the provisions of Vietnamese law.
